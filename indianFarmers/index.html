Farmer's suicides in India


The world of poverty

Too often, poverty and deprivation get covered as events. The explanation being when some disaster strikes, people die. However, poverty is about much more than starvation, deaths or near famine conditions. It is the sum total of a multiplicity of factors.

The weightage of some of these varies from region to region, society to society, culture to culture. But at the core is a fairly compact number of factors. They include not just income and calorie intake. Land, health, education, literacy, infant mortality rates and life expectancy are also some of them. Debt, assets, irrigation, drinking water, sanitation and jobs count too.


Agrarian crisis in India

India is an agrarian country with around 70% of its people depending directly or indirectly upon agriculture. Nearly 300,000 farmers have committed suicide since 1995, driven by debt and distress. Farmer suicides account for 13.9% of all suicides in India out of which 84.6% are males. 836 million Indians live on less than 50 cents a day. Yet the mainstream media hardly reflects this reality.

Various reasons have been offered to explain why farmers commit suicide in India, including: floods, drought, debt, use of genetically modified seed, public health, use of lower quantity pesticides due to less investments producing a decreased yield and also government economic policies.

In 2006, indebtedness (87%) and deterioration in the economic status (74%) were found to be major risk factors for farmer's suicide. Insufficient or risky credit systems, the difficulty of farming semi-arid regions, poor agricultural income, absence of alternative income opportunities, a downturn in the urban economy which forced non-farmers into farming, and the absence of suitable counselling services are few of the major causes of farmers driving to depression and committing suicide.

As much as 79.5% of India's farmland relies on flooding during monsoon season, so inadequate rainfall can cause droughts, making crop failure more common. Agricultural regions that have been affected by droughts have subsequently seen their suicide rates increase.


To make society better, we must know it better!

Subsidies offered by developed countries to its farmers play a major role in how the lives are affected all across the globe. It is perhaps time to stop concerning ourselves with regional politics and concern ourselves with global standards of well-being. Therefore, we must contemplate policies that would place the poorest and weakest person on the planet in greater control of his life.
